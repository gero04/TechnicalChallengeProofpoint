# Technical Challenge - Part A: Questions

## 1. Describe the most relevant learnings from programming-related subjects during your career.
In my career, I've found many opportunities to take a good dive in the programming-related world. I would like to describe what I consider the most profound and rich, in terms of learning, programming-related subjects in my career

Last year, my first approach to coding was in my first year, while I was taking the "Algoritmos y Estructuras de Datos", or Algorithms and Data Structures course. In this path, I've learnt the basics on various data structures, varying from the simplest, such as variables and data types, to more complex ones, such as two dimensional data arrays. I've also learnt how to create, open, modify and save both binary and CSV files, and after the course I pursued on continuing this learning path, making Python my preferred language for solving daily problems, and for more complex tasks that provided enormous volumes of data, which needed to be processed in efficient ways

On my second year, I took a course on operating systems which was called "Sistemas Operativos". In this course, I discovered the never-ending world of Linux, and I set myself the challenge of installing Linux natively on my laptop, and experiment, on my own and on my own computer, with what the subject was teaching me. At the end of the year, I could 
- Read into the main design aspects of most commercial operating systems
- Understand and apply the algorithms used by operating systems for resource management
- Install and manage OS in both physical and virtual environments
- Comprehend the characteristics and operation of distributed and real-time processing
- Identify aspects related to security and protection in various OS, in relation with the resources it manages

Overall, I think this experience pushed me to start learning on my own, ending on 4 years of daily Linux usage, and many personal projects which involved knowledge I acquired in this course

Last year (2025), I took the "Diseño de Sistemas de Información" or Information Systems Design course. I think this was the most comprehensive course I've taken on my entire career. The main goal of this subject was to teach the entire software development lifecycle, from gathering client requirements to delivering a fully functional information system. Throughout the year, I learnt about the different architectural designs that the industry of software development had used since its firsts years, ranging from purely monolithic to microservices solutions. I also took a deep dive and interest in most design principles (such as the S.O.L.I.D. principles), as I knew these where the core ideas of the software development world. With all this knowledge I gathered, I carried out a group project throughout the year and when the year came to the end, I passed the subject with no complications, and was invited to teach the subject by the University department, due to my performance.

Taken together, these experiences have given me a solid foundation in algorithms, system thinking, and software architecture, skills that are directly applicable to the data cleaning and deduplication tasks in this technical challenge.

## 2. Describe a situation you had to resolve with a teammate from a study or work group.
As I mentioned in the previous point, in the Information Systems Design course we had a project that lasted the entire academic year. I had to complete a project in which we were given the requirements and context for an information system, and together with my group we had to make it through the different steps of software development. I think this experience was really rich, because not only I had to learn by myself programming languages such as Java, Javascript and SQL, but I also had to apply what we were learning in the course, and lead my study group into successfully developing and deploying the app on 2 opportunities (the first one was a demo, with mocked data, and the second one was a complete web app with storage persistance). I took the role of the leader of the group, managing the time schedules of my teammates, discussing various strategies and problems we encountered while developing with my professors, and achieving excellent grades and performance in both deploy opportunities. I value this situation the most, between all the situations in which I had to resolve a task with a teammate from a study or work group, because I had the opportunity to work side by side with excellent study partners, and not only lead them to success, but also learn from the mistakes I made in the development process, gathering new soft skills like leadership, communication, organization and resilience
